Who Are Your Customers?

How well do you know your customer? Do you know what they think about your company and it’s products or services?

Answering “people who pay us money” is a cop out. Describing your customers by demographics isn't good enough.

If you’re going after “25-35 year old women”, your focus isn’t, well, focused.

What you need to know is:

  1. What do they believe?
  2. Who do they trust?
  3. What are they afraid of and who do they love?
  4. What are they seeking?
  5. Who are their friends?
  6. What do they talk about?
